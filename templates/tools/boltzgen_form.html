About BoltzGen

What it wraps

Boltz-1 / Boltz-2 (Wohlwend et al., MIT 2024). Joint structure + sequence generation in an OpenFold-style architecture with native support for modified residues, glycans, and non-canonical amino acids. Repo: jwohlwend/boltz.

When to use

Pick BoltzGen when your target involves glycans, post-translational modifications, or non-canonical residues that AlphaFold-based tools cannot represent. For standard protein-only targets, BindCraft or RFantibody are faster and cheaper.

Prerequisites

  • Target structure as .pdb, .cif, or .mmcif
  • Chain ID of the target within that structure
  • Optional hotspot residues (comma-separated indices)
  • Optional modification annotations (glycan sites, PTMs)

Input glossary

  • Modified residues — residues carrying a covalent modification (phosphorylation, methylation, glycan attachment). Boltz represents these natively; AlphaFold2 substitutes the nearest canonical amino acid.
  • Glycan attachment — N-linked or O-linked glycan chains on target residues. Many receptor and membrane-protein epitopes are glycan-shielded; designs that ignore glycans often fail in binding assays.
  • Hotspot residues — target-chain indices the binder should contact. Optional for BoltzGen; leave blank for unconstrained sampling.
  • Binder length — min and max residue count for generated binders. Boltz samples lengths uniformly in this window.
  • Budget — number of final candidates returned after scoring and filtering.
