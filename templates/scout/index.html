How epitopes are scored

Each surface patch is ranked by a composite score (0–1) combining five structural determinants:

30% Hydrophobic exposure
Hydrophobicity × mean RSA. Accessible hydrophobic surface drives desolvation ΔG. Chothia (1974); Vajda et al. (2018)
20% Structural order
Independent secondary structure score. β-strands (1.0) and α-helices (0.8) are strongly preferred — they are rigid, geometrically regular, and dominate natural PPI interfaces. Loops score 0.2: flexible regions may rearrange in solution, causing binder design failure. Lo Conte et al. (1999); Fleishman et al. (2011)
20% Rigidity
Inverted Z-scored backbone B-factor. Rigid, well-ordered epitopes incur lower entropic penalty upon binding. Carugo & Argos (1997)
15% Surface accessibility
Inverted burial depth. Flat, moderately curved surfaces score high — the optimal topology for shape complementarity. Deep pockets and narrow crevices are penalized. Lo Conte et al. (1999); Lawrence & Colman (1993)
15% Hot spot density
Fraction of Trp, Tyr, Arg, Phe, Lys, Asp, Glu. Core hot spots contribute >2 kcal/mol each; charged residues form salt bridges and H-bond networks. Bogan & Thorn (1998); Moreira et al. (2007)

Top 3 epitopes above quality threshold (score ≥ 0.40, ≥ 5 residues) are reported. Full data in CSV.
